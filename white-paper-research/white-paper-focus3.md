What Kind of White Papers Are Trusted by Mainstream Media (CNN / BBC)
Objective

The goal is not to produce a marketing white paper.

The goal is to produce a report that journalists from CNN, BBC, Reuters, AP, Financial Times, NYTimes, Wired and similar organizations are willing to cite as evidence.

For AI foundation models, AI SaaS products, and AI consumer applications, mainstream media rarely quote vendor marketing materials directly.

Instead, they reference reports that exhibit strong characteristics of independence, transparency, reproducibility, and public value.

2. Characteristics Shared by Frequently Cited Reports
A. Original Data

The report measures something that no one else has measured.

Examples

10,000 prompts

500 AI products

2 million benchmark runs

Large-scale user behavior

Journalists rarely cite summaries.

They prefer first-party evidence.

B. Transparent Methodology

Good reports explain

dataset

sampling

evaluation process

metrics

limitations

confidence

This allows independent verification.

C. Reproducibility

Readers should be able to reproduce similar results.

Preferred additions

GitHub

Dataset

Evaluation scripts

Raw CSV

D. Quantitative Results

Media favor

"73%"

instead of

"many"

Charts outperform adjectives.

E. Public Interest

Questions commonly covered

Which model is actually better?

Which AI hallucinates more?

Which coding assistant saves more time?

Which AI consumes more energy?

Which agent succeeds more often?

Which product leaks private data?

Journalists prioritize issues that affect readers.

F. Balanced Conclusions

High-quality reports include

Strengths

Weaknesses

Failure cases

Known limitations

Confidence intervals

Avoid claiming that a product is "the best."

Balanced analysis is more credible.

5. Implications for AI SaaS and AI Application Companies

A white paper is more likely to be cited if it answers an industry-wide question instead of promoting a single product.

Examples include:

Which AI coding assistant completes real engineering tasks most accurately?
How reliable are AI agents across multi-step workflows?
What is the real cost-quality tradeoff among leading LLM APIs?
Which multimodal models perform best on document understanding?
How often do leading AI assistants fabricate citations?
How stable are model outputs over repeated executions?

Reports framed around ecosystem-wide questions provide reusable evidence that journalists can reference regardless of which vendor they are covering.

Final Conclusion

The evidence indicates that mainstream outlets such as BBC and CNN generally favor reports that function as independent empirical research rather than marketing collateral. Across AI coverage, journalists consistently rely on sources with transparent methodology, original datasets, quantitative evidence, and reproducible evaluation procedures. Independent research institutes, academic publications, government reports, and rigorous benchmarking organizations are cited far more frequently than vendor-authored promotional materials.
